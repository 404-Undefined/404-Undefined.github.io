Grouper

The post-pandemic world needs more inclusive learning environments. Grouper is an advanced grouping algorithm that enhances classroom diversity. Teachers usually utilize random group generators to put students into groups for class activities and projects. However, we felt that this method is ineffective. The Grouper software works by taking in a list of students' names, genders, nationalities, specific academic interests and evenly distributes these students into groups based on specific parameters such as "mixed gender" and "mixed nationalities" to create a diverse learning environment. Additionally, with the onset of the coronavirus, teachers had to manually create video conference links for each group. Grouper takes group generating to another level by automatically generating Google Meet or Zoom links and emailing them to the relevant participants.

Hackathon Hosting

A Hackathon is a sprint-like event for a group of programmers to work together on a collaborative project in a short period of time. Companies such as Facebook and Google host annual Hackathons to promote creative thinking and innovation, and these events often lead to surprising, novel concepts and prototypes. The post-pandemic world needs more collaborative innovations. We provide custom frontend & backend Hackathon management websites and general assistance with hosting Hackathons.

We have successfully hosted JHack Hackathon in April 2021 where we had over 30 participants in the Asian Pacific region. Check out our Hackathon website.

Hands-Free

We are partnering with Hands Free, a UN SDG aligned, non-profit student run project (in 9 countries and growing), that, with the help of ambassadors and volunteers, produces and installs biodegradable 3D printed Hands Free solutions in our communities (schools, universities, clinics, offices, retail, places of worship, and more). They're looking for like minded students who are interested in starting a Hands Free project or chapter to provide an additional level of protection and help their communities reopen safely. They make it as easy as possible to get a project going with support every step of the way. Their solutions have an additional environmental benefit by helping reduce the consumption of single use tissues and other disposables, eliminating direct hand/finger contact with surfaces like door handles and shopping carts. To learn more or contact them please visit their Instagram: @handsfree.health or website: www.hands-free.health
